What this is

Spoke & Rail is a trip planner for people who ride bikes and take transit — and treats the bicycle as a first-class leg of the trip, not an afterthought. It compares four ways to make the same journey side by side: walk to the train, carry your bike aboard, lock your bike at the station, or dock-to-dock bikeshare.

It currently covers Philadelphia (SEPTA + PATCO + Indego), Boston (MBTA), and New York City (MTA + Metro-North + NJ Transit). More cities and routes are planned.

Why not just use Google Maps?

Google, Citymapper, and Transit all offer some version of bike+transit routing. The problem is they ignore the details that actually determine whether a trip is feasible — and whether it's worth it.

What mainstream apps do
What Spoke & Rail does
Treat transit as the only option, bikes as a footnote
Four scenarios compared on equal footing — baseline, carry, lock, bikeshare
Ignore SEPTA's bike ban during peak hours
Models the real peak rules exactly: PATCO allows bikes 24/7; SEPTA Metro and Regional Rail are off-peak weekdays only
Show total fare as if everyone pays the same
Shows your marginal cost: an Indego member's bike leg is $0, a casual rider's is $4.50 — and that changes which route wins
Route you to transit even when a direct bike ride is faster
Includes a direct bike option up to your set distance limit, with real street routing

The headline example: Mt. Airy to Collingswood is 88 min / ~$11.25 the Google way (three fares: Regional Rail + Metro + PATCO). With a personal bike carried aboard it's 75 min / ~$5.50 — biking to the BSL skips the Regional Rail fare entirely. No mainstream app will tell you that.

Who made it

Built by Joey Groton, a construction project manager and endurance cyclist in Philadelphia. The routing engine, fare modeling, and UI were developed with Claude Code.

This is an early prototype. If you find a bug, a wrong fare, or a missing route, send a note — it helps.

Prototype disclaimer. Fares are modeled as accurately as possible but some Regional Rail and commuter rail figures are estimates (shown with ~). Transit times use average headways, not live schedules. Bike and walk legs use real street geometry via OSRM but do not account for traffic signals, construction, or road conditions. Do not use this app as your only source of truth for a time-sensitive trip.
