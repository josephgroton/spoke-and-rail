What's built, what's next, and what's on the wishlist. Proposed items are things Joey has asked for; Ideas are suggestions based on what would make the biggest difference to the routing quality or geographic coverage. This is a living document - the order within each section roughly reflects priority.

Network - transit data
Shipped
Philadelphia - SEPTA + PATCO
Full network: all 13 Regional Rail lines with zone fares, Market-Frankford + Broad Street + PATCO with exact fares, surface trolleys, sample Indego docks. Peak bike rules modeled exactly (PATCO always; SEPTA Metro/RR off-peak weekdays).
Shipped
Boston - MBTA
Red, Orange, Green (B/C/D/E branches), Blue lines + Silver Line + key bus routes + commuter rail sketch. CharlieCard flat fare modeled.
Shipped
NYC - MTA Subway (L, J/Z, G)
Three lines covering Brooklyn and the L corridor into Manhattan. Bikes allowed 24/7 on NYC subway - no peak restrictions, which is modeled correctly.
Shipped
NYC - Metro-North Hudson + Harlem Lines
Hudson Line (GCT → Poughkeepsie, 10 stations) and Harlem Line (GCT → Brewster, 8 stations). Zone fares MN0–MN7, bikes off-peak only. Segment times calibrated to real timetables.
Shipped
NYC - NJ Transit NEC, Coast Line, Raritan Valley
Northeast Corridor (Penn → Trenton), North Jersey Coast Line (Penn → Bay Head), Raritan Valley Line (Penn → High Bridge). Newark Penn shared as transfer hub. Zone fares NJT0–NJT8.

NYC - LIRR (Long Island Rail Road)
Penn Station to Long Island: Port Washington, Babylon, Far Rockaway, Ronkonkoma branches. Key destinations for shore and state park cycling. Bikes off-peak only.
Proposed
NYC - Subway expansion (A/C/E, 1/2/3, 4/5/6, N/Q/R/W, B/D/F/M, 7)
The remaining major subway lines to make NYC routing complete enough to compare bike+transit for trips that don't start near the L, J/Z, or G.
Proposed
NYC - Citi Bike docks (Manhattan)
Hardcoded key dock locations across Manhattan to enable the Citi Bike scenario in NYC mode, the way Indego docks work in Philly mode.
Proposed
NYC - NJ Transit additional lines
Morris & Essex (Morristown + Gladstone branches), Montclair-Boonton, Pascack Valley, Port Jervis. Extends NJT coverage into northern NJ and the Hudson Valley.
Idea
NJ Transit River LINE
Light rail Camden → Trenton, bikes allowed at all times. Ties together the two existing networks: PATCO (Philly mode) ends in Camden; NEC (NYC mode) ends in Trenton. A cross-mode bridge trip.
Idea
Hudson-Bergen Light Rail
Bayonne → Hoboken → Weehawken → North Bergen. Bikes always allowed. Serves the Jersey City waterfront, connects to NYC ferries and PATH. Flat $2.10 fare needs a separate billing model.
Idea
NYC Ferry routes
NYC DOT Ferry is an underrated bike+transit connector - bikes always allowed, serves Astoria, Long Island City, South Brooklyn, Rockaway. Flat fare ~$4. Could unlock some compelling showcase routes.
Idea
Washington DC - WMATA Metro + Capital Bikeshare
The Metro allows bikes off-peak on all lines. Capital Bikeshare is one of the best-integrated bikeshare systems in the US. Good candidate for a fourth city given the strong bike+transit culture.
Shipped
Live Indego availability (GBFS feed)
Pulls real-time dock counts from the Indego GBFS feed on load. Map markers color-code by bikes available (green 5+, amber 1–4, red 0). The Indego route card shows the pickup dock's live count inline. Full live dock network (~170 stations) replaces the 13 hardcoded sample docks.
Trip planning
Shipped
Four scenarios with honest cost modeling
Walk + transit (baseline), bike carried aboard (with real peak rules), bike to station + lock it, and bikeshare dock-to-dock. Each shows marginal cost under the rider's profile.
Shipped
Route detail panel
Step-by-step breakdown slides in over the map when a scenario card is selected. Shows board/alight stations, stop count, wait time, and fare breakdown. Network dims to 7% opacity so the selected route reads clearly.
Proposed
Arrive-by time planning
Let users set a target arrival time. Each scenario card then shows "Leave by X:XX" and auto-flags peak restrictions based on the computed departure time. Philly first, then other cities.
Proposed
Public lands showcase routes
Handcrafted preset routes to cycling-friendly public land destinations reachable by bike + transit: Wissahickon, Valley Forge, Hudson Highlands, Sandy Hook, D&R Canal, Rockefeller State Park. The headline use case for the product.
Idea
Generalized cost optimization (time + fare tradeoff)
Expose a slider: "I'd ride X extra minutes to save $1." The router solves for a blended objective function instead of pure time. An Indego member and a casual rider would then get genuinely different routes, not just different price tags on the same route.
Idea
Permalink / shareable trip URL
Encode origin, destination, city, and profile into URL query params so a trip can be bookmarked or sent to someone. Low-effort, high-utility - especially for the public lands showcase routes.
Routing engine quality
Shipped
Time-based Dijkstra with bike-possession state
State graph encodes whether the rider currently has their bike, enabling the carry/lock scenarios without separate routing passes. Bike and walk legs use real OSRM street geometry on the selected route.
Idea
Real GTFS timetables
Biggest quality jump available. Replace average-headway waits with actual departure times from published GTFS feeds. Users could plan around the 8:24 train instead of "approximately 22 minutes from now." SEPTA, NJ Transit, Metro-North, and PATCO all publish GTFS. Requires a build step or OTP backend.
Idea
Low-stress bike routing (LTS)
Route bike legs on protected lanes and quiet streets (Level of Traffic Stress 1–2) instead of shortest path. Mainstream apps route bikes like cars; this is a meaningful differentiator. Uses OSM cycleway/lane/surface tags. Currently all bike legs use OSRM car profile with a circuity factor.
Idea
Elevation and grade penalty for bike legs
Hills are a big deal for most cyclists. Add a grade-based time penalty to bike legs using OSM elevation data or a DEM. A flat 3-mile route on the Schuylkill Trail should rank above a hilly 2.5-mile shortcut through Manayunk.
Production path
Idea
OpenTripPlanner 2 backend
Replace the hand-built Dijkstra with OTP 2 self-hosted on a VPS (~$40–80/mo for 8–16 GB RAM). OTP handles bike+transit and bikeshare natively from real GTFS + OSM. The custom work becomes the cost model, peak rules, and this UI - which is where the product differentiation actually lives.
Idea
Verified fare tables
Most Regional Rail and commuter rail fares in the app are marked ~ (estimated). Before any public launch, replace all estimated figures with verified numbers sourced directly from agency fare pages. SEPTA RR zone table, NJT fare calculator, Metro-North off-peak schedule.
